About me

I am a final-year PhD student at Cornell where I have had the privilege of being advised by David Shmoys. My research has focussed on discrete optimization problems and adjacent considerations like fairness, recourse and equipping algorithms with predictions, especially in an inventory-inspired setting. During this time I also got to spend several months at Microsoft Research India where I worked on vector retrieval based problems under the guidance of Ravishankar Krishnaswamy. Prior to this, I completed my undergraduate studies at Rice University where I won the Michael Ross Franco for undergraduate research projects supervised by Andrew Schaefer, one of which was co-supervised by Ankit Patel.
